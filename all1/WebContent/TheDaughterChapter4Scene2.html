As soon as the house was empty, she threw off her hood and cloak of rushes, and dressed in her fine clothes, went to the dance again. The master's son was at the dance again, hoping the beautiful lady would come again. When he saw Cap o' Rushes he rushed up to her, and wouldn't leave her side all evening. But as before, Cap o' Rushes would tell him nothing about herself, and slipped off before the dance was over.
