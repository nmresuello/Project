The following evening there was another dance. Again the others begged Cap o' Rushes to come with them; again she refused, but went later to the dance dressed in fine clothes.
The master's son was waiting for her, and danced with her all evening. 'I don't know who you are,' said the master's son to Cap o' Rushes that evening.
'But if I lose you, I will pine away and die for you.' And he gave her a ring to put on her finger, to remember him by.
